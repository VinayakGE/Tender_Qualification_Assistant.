## Decision Frequency Guide

| Rating | Definition | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| High | Evaluates tenders daily or weekly — core business activity | Higher volume = more varied reasoning per session; more likely to have observed edge cases |
| Medium | Monthly evaluation cycle — bid on specific projects | Enough volume to have developed decision heuristics |
| Low | Occasional — tenders are not the primary activity | Fewer decisions observed per session; still valuable if sector coverage is rare |

A consultant evaluating 100 tenders/year produces more varied reasoning than a contractor evaluating 5. Weight Decision Frequency at shortlisting when two prospects are otherwise equivalent.

## Session Prep Protocol

Before each confirmed session:

1. Ask for 2–3 tenders they evaluated recently. Redact confidential details if needed.
2. Run those tenders through the engine before the meeting. Record output privately.
3. During the session: let them walk through their reasoning first. Do not show engine output.
4. After they conclude: show engine output, compare step by step.

Record per session: Reasoning sequence · Conclusion and primary reason · Recommendation agreement · Reasoning agreement class · New signals surfaced · Trust observations (agreed but wouldn't act, skipped sections, wouldn't present to manager).
