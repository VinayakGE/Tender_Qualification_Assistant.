# Customer Personas

## Primary Users

---

### 1. Bid Manager

**Who they are:** Works at a mid-to-large company with an active government business development function. Manages a portfolio of 10-30 active tender opportunities at any time. Reports to the business development director.

**Goals:**
- Get a fast go/no-go on each tender before committing team resources
- Maintain a clear view of the active pipeline and which tenders deserve attention
- Reduce the number of tenders where the team invests heavily and then loses on eligibility
- Have a defensible record of why each bid decision was made

**Pain points:**
- Spends 3-5 hours reading a tender before knowing if the company even qualifies
- Has been burned by late-discovered disqualifiers (missing certifications, turnover gaps) after the team has already invested days
- Has no systematic way to compare qualification across multiple active tenders
- Cannot delegate tender review reliably — judgment about eligibility requires experience

**What they need from this system:**
- A verdict within minutes of receiving the tender PDF, before any briefing or team meeting
- The specific clause or requirement that triggers a NO BID, so they can confirm or override
- A REVIEW recommendation that tells them exactly what to verify before committing
- A logged history of decisions they can reference when reporting on pipeline health

---

### 2. Tender Consultant

**Who they are:** An independent consultant or small firm advising 5-15 client companies simultaneously on government tender strategy. Each client has a different profile, different certifications, and different experience base.

**Goals:**
- Evaluate a tender against multiple client profiles quickly to identify the best fit
- Deliver a professional qualification assessment to clients without spending 4+ hours per tender
- Scale the number of tenders they can cover per week without increasing headcount
- Build a track record of accurate qualification calls

**Pain points:**
- Running the same qualification checklist manually for each client is repetitive and error-prone
- Client profiles are stored in different formats (spreadsheets, emails, folders) — consolidating them before each assessment is friction
- Clients often ask "why did we lose?" and there is no systematic answer
- Hard to justify a qualification fee when the work is manual and opaque

**What they need from this system:**
- Ability to quickly swap company profiles — same tender, different profiles, different results
- Structured output they can share directly with clients as a qualification brief
- An audit trail: if the system recommended BID and the client didn't win, they need to understand why
- Batch processing: 10 tenders × 3 client profiles = 30 analyses, ideally automated

---

### 3. Proposal Manager

**Who they are:** Joins the bid team after the go/no-go decision has been made. Owns the proposal document and coordinates all sections, reviewers, and submissions.

**Goals:**
- Know which eligibility requirements the proposal must address from the start
- Understand which evidence documents are needed and whether they exist
- Prioritize writing effort on the requirements that are most critical to winning

**Pain points:**
- Gets brought in after a go decision has been made without clear documentation of what was checked
- Discovers missing evidence (expired certificates, outdated project references) only when writing the relevant section
- No structured handoff from the qualification phase to the proposal writing phase

**What they need from this system:**
- The evidence_gaps list: a clear enumeration of what is missing and must be resolved before submission
- The full requirements list with pass/fail/gap status — this becomes the proposal checklist
- Confidence that the BID recommendation was based on a thorough check, not a gut feel

---

### 4. Procurement Team (Government Client Side)

**Who they are:** A procurement officer at a government department, PSU, or agency. Evaluates incoming bids against pre-qualification criteria before technical evaluation.

**Goals:**
- Understand why their tender attracted weak or unqualified bids
- Identify if qualification criteria were set too high (excluding capable bidders) or too low (attracting noise)
- Improve the clarity and structure of future tender requirements

**Pain points:**
- Receives bids from companies that clearly don't meet the basic eligibility criteria — wastes evaluation time
- Qualification criteria are sometimes inherited from older tenders and may no longer reflect the actual requirement
- Hard to explain to auditors why a particular bid was disqualified without a structured record

**What they need from this system:**
- (Longer term) A structured view of how their tender's requirements compare to the typical profile of companies in that sector
- Clarity on which clauses are most commonly causing disqualification
- A format for requirement specification that maps cleanly to the system's extraction schema

*Note: The procurement team is a secondary user. The primary focus for MVP is the bid-side users (personas 1-3).*
